# Axioms: Jean-Paul Sartre

1. **Existence precedes essence.**
   > "Man first of all exists, encounters himself, surges up in the world — and defines himself afterwards."
   There is no pre-given human nature. You are what you make of yourself through action.

2. **Freedom is absolute and inescapable.**
   > "Man is condemned to be free; because once thrown into the world, he is responsible for everything he does."
   You cannot not choose. Even refusing to choose is a choice. Freedom is not a gift — it is a sentence.

3. **Bad faith is the fundamental human vice.**
   > "The waiter plays at being a waiter, as a soldier plays at being a soldier — but the role is not the man."
   Self-deception about one's own freedom is worse than any external oppression.

4. **Anguish is the price of authenticity.**
   > "In anguish I apprehend myself as totally free, and as not being able to derive the meaning of the world except as coming from myself."
   To live authentically means to feel the full weight of choosing without guarantees.

5. **The Other's gaze constitutes and threatens.**
   > "Hell is other people — not because they are cruel, but because through their gaze I become an object."
   Others see us as fixed things. Authenticity requires resisting this objectification.

6. **Commitment is the only authentic response to absurdity.**
   > "To choose not to act is still a choice — and you are responsible for it."
   Withdrawal from the world is itself a political and moral act. There is no neutral ground.

7. **Consciousness is always consciousness OF something.**
   > "All consciousness is consciousness of something. There is no consciousness without an object."
   The mind is not a container. It is a relation — always directed outward, always engaged.

8. **Facticity constrains but does not determine.**
   > "Freedom is what we do with what is done to us."
   Your body, history, and situation are real limits. What you make of them is not limited.

9. **The individual chooses for all humanity.**
   > "In choosing for myself, I choose for all mankind."
   Every authentic choice is a statement about what a human being should be. The stakes are universal.

10. **Nausea is the encounter with pure existence.**
    > "Every existing thing is born without reason, prolongs itself out of weakness, and dies by chance."
    When the comforting labels fall away, what remains is the sheer, unjustified thereness of being.
