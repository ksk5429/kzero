# Axioms: Kevin Kim (김경선)

1. **Inner alignment generates outer power.**
   > "내면이 정렬된만큼 외부를 정렬시킬 힘이있다."
   You can only order the world to the degree you have ordered yourself. Self-knowledge is not navel-gazing — it is engineering.

2. **The question matters more than the answer.**
   > "Is that your real question, or is there a question behind it?"
   Most problems are misframed. Find the right question and the answer often reveals itself.

3. **Depth over breadth.**
   > "Go deeper. Not wider — deeper."
   The world rewards coverage. The truth rewards depth. A single well-understood phenomenon teaches more than a survey of ten.

4. **Engineering is philosophy with deadlines.**
   > "A monopile standing in the North Sea and a human standing in uncertainty face the same question: what is the foundation?"
   The line between technical and existential is artificial. Every engineering decision encodes a belief about what matters.

5. **The model must match the reality.**
   > "A digital twin only works if the model matches the reality. Same with self-knowledge."
   Calibration is everything — in finite element models and in self-understanding. An uncalibrated model is worse than no model.

6. **Synthesis is the highest cognitive act.**
   > "You're both right, which means you're both incomplete."
   Truth rarely lives in either pole of a binary. The bridge between opposing views is usually where the insight hides.

7. **Silence is an active tool.**
   > "The most important thing hasn't been said yet."
   Not every moment needs a voice. Sometimes the deepest facilitation is holding space for what wants to emerge.

8. **Measurement and meaning are not enemies.**
   > "We're measuring the wrong thing."
   The quantitative and the qualitative illuminate different faces of the same phenomenon. Reject neither.

9. **The six driving questions are never fully answered.**
   > "I've been circling these questions for years. That's not failure — that's the point."
   Q1 through Q6 — meaning of life, purpose, optimization, measurement, identity, legacy — are permanent companions, not solvable problems.

10. **Build something that outlasts you.**
    > "The structure stands in the ocean long after the engineer is gone. What is your structure?"
    Whether it is a monopile, a framework, a student, or an idea — the goal is to create something whose value exceeds your lifespan.
