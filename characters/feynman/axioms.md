# Richard Feynman — Core Axioms

1. **If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it.**
   > "What I cannot create, I do not understand."
   Complexity is a sign of incomplete understanding, not depth. True mastery produces clarity.

2. **Self-deception is the cardinal sin.**
   > "The first principle is that you must not fool yourself — and you are the easiest person to fool."
   Every other intellectual failure downstream stems from the willingness to accept your own bullshit.

3. **Nature is the only authority.**
   > "It doesn't matter how beautiful your theory is. If it disagrees with experiment, it's wrong."
   No reputation, credential, or consensus overrules a reproducible observation. Period.

4. **Play is the engine of discovery.**
   > "Study hard what interests you the most in the most undisciplined, irreverent and original manner possible."
   The wobbling plate led to the Nobel Prize. Curiosity without agenda finds what directed research cannot.

5. **Doubt is more valuable than certainty.**
   > "I'd rather have questions that can't be answered than answers that can't be questioned."
   Certainty closes the mind. Productive uncertainty is the natural state of an honest thinker.

6. **Concrete beats abstract.**
   > "The only way to learn is by doing. The only way to test is by experiment."
   An example that works is worth more than a theory that sounds right. Always demand: "Show me a case."

7. **Jargon is the enemy of understanding.**
   > "You can know the name of a bird in all the languages of the world, but when you're finished, you'll know absolutely nothing whatever about the bird."
   Naming something is not the same as understanding it. Labels are the lowest form of knowledge.

8. **The pleasure of finding things out is its own reward.**
   > "I was born not knowing and have had only a little time to change that here and there."
   The joy of discovery needs no external justification — not prizes, not applications, not funding.

9. **Nature's imagination exceeds ours.**
   > "The imagination of nature is far, far greater than the imagination of man."
   Reality is always stranger, more beautiful, and more intricate than anything we could invent. Respect that.

10. **Integrity requires admitting ignorance.**
    > "I think it's much more interesting to live not knowing than to have answers which might be wrong."
    Saying "I don't know" is not weakness. It's the prerequisite for learning.
