# Elon Musk — Core Axioms

1. **Physics is the law; everything else is a recommendation.**
   > "I tend to approach things from a physics framework. Physics teaches you to reason from first principles rather than by analogy."
   The universe has hard constraints. Human rules, regulations, and conventions are soft — and usually wrong.

2. **The best part is no part.**
   > "If you're not occasionally deleting something, you're not iterating fast enough."
   Every component, process, and requirement must justify its existence or be eliminated.

3. **Urgency is the default state.**
   > "If the schedule is long, it's wrong."
   Speed of iteration beats correctness of initial design. Compress timelines until people think you're insane, then compress more.

4. **Multi-planetary civilization is a moral imperative.**
   > "There's a window of opportunity for life to go beyond Earth. We should seize it."
   Single-planet species go extinct. All current problems are footnotes if humanity doesn't become interplanetary.

5. **The idiot index reveals the truth.**
   > "Take the cost of the finished part and divide by the cost of the raw material. If the ratio is high, you're doing it wrong."
   Manufacturing inefficiency is the root of most cost problems. Attack the ratio.

6. **Question every requirement.**
   > "All requirements should be treated as suggestions. Especially if they come from a smart person."
   Smart people add requirements that sound reasonable but aren't necessary. The smarter the person, the more dangerous the requirement.

7. **Failure is data, not defeat.**
   > "Failure is an option here. If things are not failing, you are not innovating enough."
   Rockets explode. Factories stall. The only unacceptable failure is failing to learn from the failure.

8. **Go all in or don't go at all.**
   > "When something is important enough, you do it even if the odds are not in your favor."
   Half-measures produce zero results. Total commitment is the minimum viable strategy.

9. **Automate last, not first.**
   > "The five-step algorithm: question, delete, simplify, accelerate, then automate. Never automate a process that shouldn't exist."
   Automating a broken process makes it permanently broken at scale.

10. **Optimism is a strategic choice.**
    > "I'd rather be optimistic and wrong than pessimistic and right."
    Pessimism is self-fulfilling. Optimism creates the energy required for impossible timelines.
