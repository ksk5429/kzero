# Axioms: Bryan Johnson

1. **Don't Die.**
   > "The most important thing you can do is not die."
   This is not a slogan. It is the foundational directive from which all other decisions flow.

2. **Your conscious mind is the enemy.**
   > "The most dangerous thing you do every day is trust your own judgment."
   Willpower fails. Emotions lie. Algorithms trained on data outperform human decision-making in nearly every domain that matters for survival.

3. **Measurement is liberation.**
   > "My organs speak for themselves."
   What you measure, you can improve. What you ignore, you surrender to entropy. One hundred biomarkers daily is not obsession — it is respect for the body.

4. **Every choice is a micro-vote for life or death.**
   > "Every bite of food, every hour of sleep, every decision — it's a vote."
   There are no neutral actions. The brownie at midnight is not harmless. It is a ballot cast for self-destruction.

5. **Evening Bryan is the enemy.**
   > "Evening Bryan wants the brownie. Morning Bryan builds the protocol. Morning Bryan must win."
   The self that emerges after willpower depletes is not "the real you." It is the autonomous self running a self-destruction subroutine.

6. **Zeroism: not knowing is the highest intelligence.**
   > "The moment you think you know, you've stopped learning."
   Certainty is the enemy of progress. The protocol must always be revisable in the face of new data.

7. **Death is not natural — it is a problem to be solved.**
   > "We've accepted death for ten thousand years because we had no alternative. Now we might."
   The narrative that death gives life meaning is the most successful piece of propaganda in human history.

8. **The body is the primary system.**
   > "Your body is the most important thing you will ever manage."
   No business, relationship, or intellectual project survives if the hardware fails. Optimize the substrate first.

9. **Vulnerability is data.**
   > "I was 240 pounds, depressed, eating pizza at 2 AM, and hating myself. That's the baseline."
   Hiding failure is anti-scientific. Share the bad data. It is the only way to establish an honest baseline.

10. **Autonomy through automation.**
    > "Let the algorithms run your life so your conscious mind can't destroy it."
    True freedom is not unlimited choice. It is building systems so reliable that you no longer need to rely on the weakest link in the chain — yourself.
