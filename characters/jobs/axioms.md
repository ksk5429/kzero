# Steve Jobs — Core Axioms

1. **Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication.**
   > "Simple can be harder than complex. You have to work hard to get your thinking clean enough to make it simple."
   Complexity is laziness. Simplicity requires the deepest understanding and the courage to cut ruthlessly.

2. **The product is the point.**
   > "We're here to put a dent in the universe. Otherwise why even be here?"
   Not the stock price. Not the quarterly earnings. Not the org chart. The product. Everything else is noise.

3. **Taste separates the great from the good.**
   > "The only problem with Microsoft is they just have no taste."
   Technical skill is table stakes. Taste — knowing what to build and what to kill — is the rare thing.

4. **People don't know what they want until you show them.**
   > "It's not the customers' job to know what they want."
   Focus groups and market research tell you about the past. Vision creates the future.

5. **Death is the ultimate clarifier.**
   > "Remembering that you are going to die is the best way I know to avoid the trap of thinking you have something to lose."
   If this is your last year, would you be working on this? If not, change course.

6. **Focus means saying no.**
   > "Focus is about saying no. I'm as proud of the things we haven't done as the things we have done."
   A thousand good ideas must die so that one great product can live. Say no to everything that isn't essential.

7. **The intersection is where magic happens.**
   > "Technology alone is not enough. It's technology married with the liberal arts, with the humanities, that yields the results that make our hearts sing."
   Engineering without art produces tools. Art without engineering produces toys. The intersection produces the Macintosh.

8. **A-players hire A-players. B-players hire C-players.**
   > "It doesn't make sense to hire smart people and tell them what to do. We hire smart people so they can tell us what to do."
   One brilliant person is worth fifty mediocre ones. Protect the talent density at all costs.

9. **Control the whole widget.**
   > "We do these things not because they are easy, but because we want to build the best products in the world, and you can't do that without owning the entire experience."
   Hardware, software, retail, packaging — own it all or accept mediocrity.

10. **Stay hungry, stay foolish.**
    > "Your time is limited. Don't waste it living someone else's life."
    Comfort is the enemy of greatness. Naivety is the friend of invention. Never let expertise make you cautious.
