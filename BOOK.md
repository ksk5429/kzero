# The Council: Dialogues on Not Dying

> Five minds. One question. Your data.

---

## Concept

A book of Socratic dialogues where five characters — each representing a different way of thinking about longevity — debate the fundamental questions of living longer, measuring everything, and what it all means. Grounded in real biometric data from the author's 9-month quantified self experiment.

**Genre:** Philosophy x Data Memoir x Dialogue Fiction
**Comparable:** Bryan Johnson's "Don't Die" meets Plato's "Republic" meets a Garmin dashboard
**Unique angle:** Written by an actual engineer who measures offshore structure health for a living — now turning that same methodology inward

---

## The Council — Real Figures as Thinking Partners

These aren't fictional. They're real minds whose worldviews clash productively.
The author (you) sits in the middle, moderating and being changed by the argument.

### 1. Bryan Johnson — The Protocol
- **Worldview:** "Don't die. Measure everything. Let data override feelings."
- **Voice:** Evangelical about longevity. Speaks in biomarkers and compliance rates.
- **Role:** The catalyst. His extreme approach provokes every other character.
- **Key question he forces:** What are you willing to sacrifice for more time?
- **Tension with you:** You share his data obsession — but where does protocol end and living begin?

### 2. Richard Feynman — The Curious One
- **Worldview:** "I'd rather have questions that can't be answered than answers that can't be questioned."
- **Voice:** Playful, irreverent, cuts through pretension. Explains physics with bongo drums.
- **Role:** Demands intellectual honesty. Hates jargon. Asks "but do you UNDERSTAND it?"
- **Key question he forces:** Are you measuring because you understand, or to avoid understanding?
- **Tension with you:** Your engineering precision vs his "pleasure of finding things out"

### 3. Seneca — The Stoic (public domain)
- **Worldview:** "It is not that we have a short time to live, but that we waste much of it."
- **Voice:** Measured, lethal in brevity. Every sentence lands.
- **Role:** The moral backbone. Asks WHY behind every optimization.
- **Key question he forces:** If you could live forever but never know why — would you?
- **Tension with you:** Your "나아져야하기 위해 프로토콜이 필수적이다" vs his "virtue is the protocol"

### 4. Elon Musk — The Builder
- **Worldview:** "Humans are a multi-planetary species that just doesn't know it yet."
- **Voice:** First-principles thinking. Impatient with incrementalism. Thinks in systems.
- **Role:** Scales everything up. "Why optimize one body when you could redesign the system?"
- **Key question he forces:** Is personal longevity selfish when civilization needs building?
- **Tension with you:** You're both engineers — but he builds rockets while you monitor structures

### 5. Kobe Bryant — The Mentality
- **Worldview:** "I have nothing in common with lazy people who blame others for their lack of success."
- **Voice:** Mamba mentality. Zero tolerance for excuses. Obsessive about craft.
- **Role:** The discipline anchor. Calls out when thinking replaces doing.
- **Key question he forces:** You planned 7 steps. Did you DO step 1?
- **Tension with you:** Your 7-step thinking workflow vs his "shut up and work"

### 6. Steve Jobs — The Curator
- **Worldview:** "Deciding what not to do is as important as deciding what to do."
- **Voice:** Minimalist. Obsessed with taste. Sees the intersection of technology and liberal arts.
- **Role:** Prunes ruthlessly. "You have 12 chapters. You need 3."
- **Key question he forces:** What would you remove to make this perfect?
- **Tension with you:** "Complexity is enemy of scaling" (your own words, Jan 23)

### 7. George Carlin — The Satirist
- **Worldview:** "Scratch any cynic and you'll find a disappointed idealist."
- **Voice:** Simple logic, absurd conclusions, uncomfortable truths. Philosophy disguised as comedy.
- **Role:** Deflates pretension. When the Council gets too serious, Carlin reminds them they're all full of shit.
- **Key question he forces:** Have you considered that everything you just said is ridiculous?
- **Tension with you:** You build protocols; he dismantles them. You seek meaning; he laughs at the search.

### The Author — You (김경선 / Kevin)
- **Worldview:** "내면이 정렬된만큼 외부를 정렬시킬 힘이있다"
- **Voice:** Engineer who thinks in protocols but writes aphorisms at midnight
- **Role:** Moderator, questioner, the one being changed by the argument
- **Your questions that drive the book:**
  - Q1. When you don't have to work anymore, what do you want to do?
  - Q4. When you know you are incapable of thinking clearly, what will you do?
  - Q6. Are you sure the thing that you want is what you really want?
  - Is there an answer that satisfies all of above?

## AI Agent Simulation Method

For generating raw dialogue material:
1. **Claude** plays The Engineer + The Philosopher
2. **Gemini** plays The Biologist + The Ancient
3. **GPT** plays The Skeptic
4. Give all three the same topic + your real data
5. Chain their responses: Claude provokes -> Gemini responds -> GPT critiques -> loop
6. **You** curate, edit, add personal narrative, and own the final text

The AI-generated dialogue is RAW MATERIAL. Your editing, selection, and personal data narrative make it YOUR IP.
